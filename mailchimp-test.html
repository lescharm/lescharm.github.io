T&O Consultant Michael Jonckheere Pitches in with Habitat for Humanity
I’m excited to share highlights from my recent Habitat for Humanity (HFH) volunteer experience in El Salvador. For those that are not familiar with HFH, their mission is to mobilize volunteers and community partners in building affordable housing and promoting homeownership as a means to breaking the cycle of poverty.
 
Along with a team of eight Canadians from all sorts of backgrounds, I helped dig and pour the cement foundation for a new home near the capital, San Salvador. The most memorable part was meeting the family that will soon live in the home. Seven members, spanning four generations from 3-year-old Valentina to her 83-year-old “gran abuela and abuelo” (great grandparents) were immensely appreciative for the help we gave.
 
The great grandparents spent their entire adult lives as proprietors of a tobacco plantation for minimal pay other than food and onsite housing. With the great grandmother now blind and immobile, they are unable to work and, as a result, cannot afford to stay. The rest of the family barely makes ends meet with their piñata business and needed a place to house everyone including their elders. While most homes in the area take about nine months to build, our contribution reduced that to three months and helped ensure completion before the family’s Christmas.
 
Although the sole purpose of the build was to benefit the family and their community, everyone involved, including myself, felt rewarded in some way. With every shovel full of dirt, gravel, and concrete (and there were many!) I felt like I was making a difference for an amazing group of people. Since most of the family worked alongside us all week, we were able to build a relationship with them that crossed the language and cultural barriers. I could see the positive impact my efforts had on them. They were glowing all week. Much to the delight of the local children, the family even took the time to build us a piñata which we happily cracked open to share the spoils. Overall, we couldn’t have asked for a more generous and deserving group of people.
 
The build was an experience I will carry with me for a long time. Was the hard work, heat, time, and effort worth it? Without question. I’m already looking forward to joining another project in 2017.
